  • What do you do when you’re not adventuring?

    Fabien bounces around to various normal odd jobs to get cash. Things that don't show off his skills, like working as a dock hand, or a dish washer. Average punch-clock joe stuff.

    Why is that not what you do all the time?

    He maintains a low profile to avoid former handlers looking to drag him back for one more job, or in case he's ever burned and some former victims come looking. He's trained in how to stay off the radar, so he's not taking home prize money from archery contests. He also keeps an ear to the ground in case any word pops up about his lifelong target.

    Previous career set him up with enough gear that he could sell and retire in comfort. But he needs to stay sharp just in case. He might get a revenge lead that would cause him to just walk off a job and leave the city for a bit, or go on a stakeout, or whatever. So far, they've all turned up to be useless, and he shows back up at some other job. He also just can't say no to family, and the few close friends might as well be family.. See #4, #5 for more detail).

    Why would you make a bad decision?

    Aside from his own revenge. Going freelance, and keeping an ear out in low places means he hears stories. Stories about people who have been wronged. The sort of people who just won't ever be in a position to do something about it. Fabien is a sucker for nearly anyone with the right sad story. Some son or daughter who's poor father was imprisoned for some imagined insult to a noble? He'll take that job pro-bono. They don't always end in murder. Sometimes a creative punishment is more fitting, but it's coming. That's what he's willing to risk exposure for. That and…

    What do you do when you’re not adventuring?
    In terms of employment, Garak is in “private security services”, mainly for Silverside concerns. It’s gig work (more in #3), and a lot of its challenges get solved without violence (there’s a reason he’s proficient in three core social skills). He’s also a “friend of the neighborhood” to a few areas in Silverside, especially where he lives; in particular he’s known to a few street performers who know he’s a reliable talent at playing the straight man to their japery, and his presence helps to draw the passing eye to their performances.

    Why is that not what you do all the time?

    Why is that not what you do all the time?
    Professionally, Skend has a particular set of skills for particular situations, but the need for someone at his level very much ebbs and flows. So sometimes he’s just not under contract. Other times he lets his sense of right & wrong get in the way of doing something for money, though experience has tempered that a bit. Considering that it’s his military career that’s the main part of why he’s a level 15 character, that experience runs deep and with a fair bit of history, and sometimes tells him it’s better to walk away than get involved. Sometimes he even listens.

    The Emirates are involved and up to no good like usual, or the people’s freedoms are on the line! Sometimes that’s the same thing.

Raised in a Chultan-Mulhorand family of traveling archeologists, Shards has quested for knowledge and to return ancient artifacts to their rightful inheritors - except to people like the Thay, screw those guys. But then her life's work, and spending time with her friends and family, was derailed by bonding with an artifact that has other plans...
  • Drive: Unwanted Destiny.
    Without that, she’d be a Big Damn Hero, but in a scholarly, archeological, thieving sort of way. The Key has a plan. It must open things, and so she must open those things. It was easier when she was younger and more foolish: she’d always hated locked and closed doors, boxes, chests…but the key makes it a compulsion, until it has been fully assembled, and can unlock whatever it is meant to free. She has had it long enough to know the consequences for being attuned to the Key can sometimes outweigh its benefits. To be honest, she sometimes takes what she hopes are beneficial thieving jobs (steal from bad guys) because otherwise the Key makes her antsy. Shards hopes whatever it wants to free in the end is not horrible, and she is actively researching what it could be.

  • What do you do when you’re not adventuring?

    Shards goes on archeological digs for her own or others’ greater knowledge, to get items before the bad guys do, and return things to those that should have them. When not on a dig or mission (which are sometimes slow work and sometimes kicking action stuff), though, she works as a translator, helps appraise/historically place items, reads books, or hangs around the Silverside Museum of Ancient History, reading old scrolls and looking at what others brought in. She also likes to hang out with her family (if in town) and friends, eat good food (meat, as an obligate predator), dance, drink wine. She loves stories, telling and hearing them. She also sometimes stealthily walks the street at night, listening and watching, to calm her mind. But eventually she must feel sand trickling through her paws again and the release she feels when she opens something that has been locked up for too long.

    Why is that not what you do all the time?

    Mounting expeditions is expensive, often fruitless, occasionally dangerous, and costs a lot of money. She needs to go on them, but also needs to rest and earn a little money in between. She also likes to visit with friends and family and drink clean water, sleep in a bed, etc. And do research in the comfort of a library or museum.

    Why would you make a bad decision?

    She’s going to open something she should have left well alone. She is compelled by the Key to find its pieces and unlock many things, and sometimes that has been bad. So, in addition to following the Key’s compulsions, she is trying to race the Key so she can find out what it wants, in the end, before it succeeds with her as its instrument. She will make bad decisions for that knowledge, just as much as she is compelled to open and free things.

  • What do you do when you’re not adventuring?

    What do you do when you’re not adventuring?
    Training for the next big event, game, opportunity to compete in some kick ass action. This is often workouts, but also building or constructing new traps and obstacles to test skill against.

    Why is that not what you do all the time?

    Why is that not what you do all the time?
    Training costs $$$. Between the magical healing, travel, food, fees, contractors and costs at the gym, any prize money quicky vanishes. So Redge is spends a lot of time trying to find ways to train that also pay the bills (teaching, being a trainer); things that don't interrupt training too much to pay the bills (doing publicity events, speeches, endorecements); or more often than not things that pay too much to pass up the chance (unlicenced dungeon delving!!!)

    Why would you make a bad decision?

    Redge doesn't like to quit. If he is committed, and feels like there is even a slight chance of success he is gonna do it.

    The flip side is he feels the weight of failure very heavily, and will do most anything to not fail.

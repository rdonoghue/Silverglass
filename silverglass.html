Streets of Silverglass: The City

History

Technically, it’s two cities - The Grand Revived Free City of Sudlophor, Reborn in the Glory of the Everlasting Flame to the west and Novo Argentium to the east. That’s a mouthful, and it is most commonly shortened to Silverglass in reflection of the iconic towers that represent each city. The cities lie on opposite sides of the straights that connect the Shining Sea to the "Lake" of Steam (Actually an inland saltwater sea). Both are fairly young cities, built after the Lake of Steam's level rose back to it's previous levels, and while the geography of the area has changed, ruins from the old cities are a part of the landscape.

The western city, called "Fireside" by occupants unbound by formality, is nominally a free city ruled by its Sovereign out of the multi-colored Tower of Glass. In practice, it is accepted that the Sovereign is a puppet of the Skyfire Emirates, with just enough of a fig leaf of independence to provide the Emirates insulation from the consequences of their expansionist tendencies. Fireside is divided into an inner and outer city. The inner city is a genuine wonder, constructed by the many elementals "on loan" from the Emirates. While it sees regular trade and traffic, the only permanent residents are those highly ranked - the Genasi, Greater genasi and highly ranked mortals. Some Jinnfolk are also said to maintain residences in the city, but such matters are closely held secrets. The outer city, beyond the gates, it rather less spectacular, and serves as a home for those below the Pasha's and Beys of the Inner city.

There is a little more debate about how the eastern city, got the name "Silverside". It is most commonly attributed to the Silverlight, an immense lighthouse, visible far out into the silver sea. However, there is also a persistent rumor that The Protector is a disguised silver dragon. If true, the Protector isn't being subtle about it, incorporating silver dragons into her coat of arms and the decorations of Silverhold. While the Protector's informal authority goes largely unchallenged, the day to day ruling of the city lies in the hands of a council whose seats align with the factions and powers who sponsored the development of the city. While these factions were unified in their desire to not hand control of the neck over to the Skyfire Emirates, their politics do not otherwise tend to overlap.

Fireside

Class is of tremendous importance in Fireside, and the nature of class strongly favors the Genasi. Within skyfire, the pecking order is:

At the top of all things, are the genie. They are the absolute rulers of the Emirates, but even so, they are far from numerous. The simple truth is that only so many genie are that interested in a relatively backwater world. The genie themselves have an incredibly elaborate system of position and privilege which changes and evolves over time. As this cycle changes, so does the prevalence of different types of genie in the Emirates.

Within Fireside, the chief figure is the Sovereign, the enigmatic ruler of the city whose identity is a closely guarded secret. Some even suspect that the title changes hands with some frequency, based upon elemental politics, but there's no proof to back up that suspicion. The Sovereign's title does not exist in the Skyfire Emirates, but it equates to at least a very highly ranked Pasha, though some suspect that the Sovereign is a genie in disguise

The next rank down are the pasha, sometimes called greater genasi. Bloodline purity is of tremendous importance to many pasha, as it serves as a measure of their proximity to genie-kind, and in turn, of their own importance. Predictably, this means there is an elaborate pecking order among the pasha which is a reflection of the politics among the genie. It is possible for a prominent bey, or a powerful elemental ally to be recognized as a pasha, and such figures are treated with respect for their personal power, but they are towards the bottom of the overall pasha oeder of things. Still, the least of the pasha still has access to more power and wealth than most can dream of.

Beneath them are the bey, often viewed as the true 'citizens' of the emirates, as they have many protected rights, including those of property ownership. To be a bey is not the same guarantee of earthly success that being a pasha is, but it still offers a substantial head start. The vast majority of bey are genasi, and genasi blood is one path to guarantee bey status. A small number of bey are non-genasi who have been rewarded for exceptional service. Such bey are granted a plaque of office, and woe unto the bey who loses theirs, for it will not be replaced.

Beneath the bey are the bulk of the population, the nabob. The is a term generally used to describe all of those without elemental blood. While the term is not an insult (and there absolutely are insulting version of it), it's connotations are...not great.

Informally, the nabob are divided into man distinctions, the two most common being celeb and kir. Celeb are those nabob who bring value to the Emirates, such as skilled craftsmen or soldiers. Kir are those nabob who are seen as a drain upon the emirates - that is to say, the poor and criminal - and that term is absolutely an insult.

Organizations

The Koru - Fireside does not have a dedicated police organization, and they partner with Silverside's Hunter's Guild for most such responsibilities, but they also rely on a collection of animated statues, called "Koru", who tirelessly patrol the streets of the city. While their routes are largely planned out, the are varied enough to keep from being too predictable, and the koru posess enough intelligence and initiaitve to mix things up a little. This intelligence also allows them to answer simple question, provide directions and generally be helpful.

The exact mechanics behind the Koru are a mystery, though it's understood that they respond to the Soveriegn's command. They also speak with a single voice in a manner that suggests they may share some amount of awareness.

There is a small administrative group responsible for the upkeep and maintenence of the Koru, and it includes a small number of "Koru" officers, who have the authority to order units of Koru in situations which merit a better understanding.

Silverside

While Silverside is more of a free city than Fireside, that is not a result of a lack of outside influence so much as it is a lack of agreement among those outside influences. The council which rules the city represents interests from Tethyr, The Border Kingdome, The Steamlake Alliance, Chult and (nominally) Halruua, with other minor powers have presences as well. The presence of the city itself is almost an afterthought.

While this is not the most stable of situations, it's driven by the necessity that Novo Argentium remain a genuinely free city - should any one councilmember take too strong a position, the expectation is that the Emirates would seize upon this "imperial aggression" and take it upon themselves to "liberate" the city.
